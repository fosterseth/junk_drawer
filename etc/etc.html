Luke ch:1 verses 28-31 Luke ch 1 verses 46-55 "Mary's Canticle" Her response of the Angels message in saying "Yes". 2) The Visitation: Mary goes to visit her cousin Elizabeth and is hailed by her as "Blessed Among Women". Luke ch: 1 verses 42-45 3) The Nativity: Mary gives birth to Jesus in the stable at Bethlehem. Luke ch: 2 verses 6-7 4) The Presentation: Mary and Joseph present Jesus to His Heavenly Father in the Temple of Jerusalem for forty days after his birth. Luke ch: 2 verse 22 Simeon says to Blessed Mary "And a sword will pierce your own soul to". 5) The finding of Jesus in the Temple: After searching for 3 days, Mary and Joseph find the twelve-year-old Jesus sitting in the temple with the learned Doctors. Luke ch: 2 verses 46-47 The Sorrowful Mysteries: Are to be said on all Tuesdays, Fridays, and Sundays of Lent. 1) The Agony In The Garden: The thought of our sins and of his coming sufferings causes the agonizing Savior to sweat blood. Matt. ch: 26 verses 36-39 2) The Scourging: Jesus is stripped and unmercifully scourged until his body is one mass of bloody wounds. Matt. ch: 27 verses 25-26 3) The Crowning With Thorns: Jesus claim to Kingship is ridiculed by a crown of thorns being placed on his head and a reed in his hand. Matt. ch:27 verses 28-29 4) The Carrying Of The Cross: Jesus shoulders his own cross and carries it to the place of crucifixion, while Mary follows him sorrowing. John ch:19 verses 17-18 5) The Crucifixion: Jesus dies, nailed to the cross after 3 hours of agony witnessed by his mother. Luke.ch: 23 verses 45-46 The Glorious Mysteries : Are to be said on all Sundays (except during advent and lent) and all Wednesdays and Saturdays. 1) The Resurrection: Jesus rises from the dead on Easter Sunday Glorious, and immortal, as he had predicted. Matt. ch. 28 verses 5-6 2) The Ascension: Jesus ascends into heaven forty days after his resurrection to sit at the right hand of God, the Father, from whence he shall return to judge the living and the dead. Luke ch.24 verses 50-51 3) The Descent of the Holy Spirit: Jesus sends the Holy Spirit in the form of fiery tongues upon his Apostles and disciples. Acts ch. 2 verses 3-4 4) The Assumption: Mary's soul returns to God and her Glorified body is assumed into heaven and reunited with her soul. Rev. ch:11 verse 19 5) The coronation: Mary is crowned as queen of heaven and Earth, Queen of Angels and Saints, that she will help show others the way to her Son. Rev.ch. 12 verse 1-6 The Luminous Mysteries: Are to be prayed on all Thursdays. 1) The Baptism in Jordan.
